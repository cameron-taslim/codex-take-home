# Experiment Sample Inputs

Use these values on `/experiments/new` for fresh test runs.

All samples below are intentionally written to avoid dull, beige, or low-saturation outputs. They should push Codex toward richer contrast, a clearer color story, and a more visually finished eCommerce treatment.

## Sample 1

- Experiment name: `Spring Capsule Hero Test`
- Component type: `Hero banner`
- Target audience: `Returning shoppers looking for premium spring layering pieces with a strong editorial point of view`
- Brand tone: `Editorial`
- Brand constraints: `Keep the copy premium and product-led. Avoid discount language, countdown urgency, coupon framing, and all-caps sales messaging. Use one vivid, high-contrast color story with a confident focal CTA. Avoid beige-heavy, washed-out, or nearly monochrome styling unless absolutely necessary.`
- Seed context: `Feature lightweight outerwear, soft tailoring, and transitional layering for early spring. Emphasize texture, wardrobe longevity, and a richer spring palette such as indigo, cobalt, oxblood, ember, or deep olive paired with lighter contrast surfaces.`
- Extra prompt: `Generate one polished landing-page hero direction with a bold, premium color palette, a strong headline, supporting subheadline, concise CTA, and a visually resolved HTML preview that feels merchandised rather than minimal.`

## Sample 2

- Experiment name: `Weekend Drop Split Layout`
- Component type: `Landing page`
- Target audience: `Mobile-first shoppers browsing curated new arrivals and responding to bold, styled visual merchandising`
- Brand tone: `Confident`
- Brand constraints: `No promo banners, no coupon framing, no external images, and no gimmicky animation language. Keep it clean and conversion-oriented, but still visually rich. Use a distinct dominant accent plus one supporting color. Avoid flat white cards, pale taupe backgrounds, or low-energy neutral-on-neutral treatments.`
- Seed context: `Highlight a limited spring edit with modular outfit building, neutral foundations, and sharp color accents that make the collection feel immediate and styled. Aim for contrast, depth, and strong mobile-friendly hierarchy.`
- Extra prompt: `Create a split-layout concept with wrapped content blocks, strong hierarchy, and a premium color story that feels vivid and deliberate. The preview should look finished, high-contrast, and visually memorable inside the frame.`

## Sample 3

- Experiment name: `PDP Buy Box Trust Refresh`
- Component type: `Product detail page (PDP) buy box`
- Target audience: `High-intent shoppers comparing premium essentials before purchase and expecting a refined, high-trust visual treatment`
- Brand tone: `Minimalist`
- Brand constraints: `Keep the message calm, precise, and premium. No discount framing, no fake scarcity, and no oversized badge clutter. Minimalist should still mean rich contrast and intentional accent usage, not bland grayscale or washed-out beige. Use a crisp premium palette with one restrained but visible accent.`
- Seed context: `Support a premium cotton shirt launch with emphasis on fit, fabric quality, and easy wardrobe pairing. Favor deep inky neutrals, charcoal, forest, midnight blue, or dark espresso tones with one sharper accent to create a luxurious buy-box moment.`
- Extra prompt: `Generate a compact buy-box concept with strong trust cues, concise body copy, and a premium high-contrast palette. Keep the layout tight, but make the preview feel styled, polished, and clearly more intentional than a default product card.`

## Sample 4

- Experiment name: `Category Header New Arrivals Push`
- Component type: `Category page header`
- Target audience: `Frequent browsers scanning seasonal new arrivals on desktop and responding to warm but visually striking merchandising`
- Brand tone: `Warm`
- Brand constraints: `Keep the tone inviting but still elevated. Avoid discount-heavy language, flashing urgency, and any dependence on external assets. Use a warm, saturated palette with visible contrast and an obvious point of visual emphasis. Avoid faded neutrals, dusty beige gradients, or anything that reads timid or unfinished.`
- Seed context: `Promote a soft spring palette, versatile day-to-night dressing, and easy layering across tops, trousers, and outerwear. Use warmer premium tones such as amber, rust, merlot, terracotta, or deep blush balanced with darker anchors for depth.`
- Extra prompt: `Generate a category-header direction with a welcoming headline, supporting copy, and a warm, premium color story that feels alive and merchandised. The HTML preview should feel visually complete, not sparse or muted.`
